Reliability work has a way of scattering itself across tools. The failure data
sits in a spreadsheet, the Weibull fit happens in a one-off script, the block
diagram lives in a slide, and the maintenance interval is somebody's best guess.
**Reliafy** pulls that whole chain into a single workspace.

## What you can do today

- **Fit life distributions** — Weibull, Lognormal, Exponential, Gamma, Normal,
  and proportional-hazards models — to exact, censored, and truncated data, with
  probability plots, confidence bounds, and goodness-of-fit.
- **Build reliability block diagrams** on a drag-and-drop canvas: series,
  parallel, k-of-n, and standby redundancy, then compute system reliability,
  MTTF, importance measures, and minimal path/cut sets.
- **Decide when to act** — rank candidate distributions, compare two designs
  head-to-head, and find the cost-optimal preventive-replacement interval.

## Built around your data

Upload a CSV once and reuse it across every model you fit from it. Saved models
reopen instantly, and everything is private to your account. New accounts even
start with a few sample datasets, models, and a worked RBD so there's something
to explore on day one.
